## What is a Deep Copy?
- A deep copy not only copies the object's member variables but also creates copies of any resources that those members point to. Instead of just copying a pointer's address, it allocates new memory (or a new resource) and copies the actual data from the source into the new location.

- The Core Idea: A deep copy creates a truly independent and identical duplicate of an object. Modifying or destroying the copy has no effect on the original object.
